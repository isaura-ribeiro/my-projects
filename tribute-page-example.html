Florence Nightingale
(1820-1910)

Founder of Modern Nursing

"The Lady with the Lamp"
Florence Ninghtingale is considered the founder of modern nursing, commonly nicknamed as "Lady with the Lamp".

Here's a timeline of Nightingale's life highlights:

  • 1820 - born in Florence, Italy
  • 1844 - It took almost a decade for Nightingale to express her decision to enter the nursing field. Because of this decision, she rejected the expected role for a woman of her status to become a wife and a mother, opposing to her family and the social code at the time.
  • 1860 - Florence laid the foundation of professional nursing establishing her nursing school at St Thomas' Hospital in London, the first secular nursing school in the world and is now part of King's College London.
  • 12th May, International Nurse's Day - celebrated on her birthday, as a tribute, for her contribution for nursing career.
